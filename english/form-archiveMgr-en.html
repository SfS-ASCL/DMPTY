Quality Assurance

Quality assurance is important for the long term storage of the data. For example, it is important to ensure the readability of OCR data, the interpretability of scans, the validity of program code, etc. The strategies used for quality assurance that the project will follow and who is responsible for quality assurance must be clearly described in the data management plan.

In the area of quality assurance, CLARIN-D can only help with the data formats used. All other aspects of quality assurance are the responsibility of the project provider.

Data Organization

For data organization, the granularity of the data to be stored is a complex problem. It is important to be able to split the data into meaningful digital objects that must later be consolidated and searchable. What a meaningful digital object is, however, depends on the context of the data.

For example, a single poem or article can be a meaningful digital object for a literary analysis. On the other hand, many yearsâ€™ worth of newspaper articles are gathered in linguistic corpora. In this example, the whole corpus is a meaningful digital unit, but not its individual components.

The provider must clearly discuss the question of granularity in the data management plan. It must be clearly stated what the digital objects in the raw data are and what their granularity is.
